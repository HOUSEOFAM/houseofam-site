Effect 01

The Ink Curtain.

How it works

A gold light slash cuts diagonally across the screen — then three dark ink panels sweep in from the left in a staggered wipe, each delayed 60ms from the last. The AM mark pulses at the centre. Then all three sweep out to the right exposing the new page underneath.

It's the same technique used by the world's top luxury brand websites. Every page switch becomes a deliberate editorial moment — like turning a page in a premium magazine.

Gold slash: leads at 0ms · Panel 1: 80ms · Panel 2: 140ms · Panel 3: 200ms
Total transition: ~1.8s · Easing: cubic-bezier(.76,0,.24,1) — industry standard luxury ease

Effect 02

Word By Word.

How it works

Every heading is split into individual words. Each word is wrapped in an overflow:hidden clip — so it's invisible until it rises. On page entry, each word rises from translateY(105%) with a subtle 3D tilt (rotateX(-20deg)), staggered 75ms per word.

The result: the heading is performed rather than loaded. The eye follows each word left to right before settling on the full message. Every page entry feels like a reveal.

Effect 03

Magnetic Pull.

How it works

The cursor is a gold dot with an SVG ring that spins the text "EXPERIENCE · HOUSE OF AM ·" around it at all times. When you hover any interactive element, the ring expands and the text fades in fully — revealing itself like a brand stamp.

Elements themselves have a magnetic field: within 80px, they pull toward your cursor at 35% strength. Move slowly and deliberately — it's a reward for attention.

How it works

Two gold rows run simultaneously — top row scrolls left→right, bottom row scrolls right→left at a slightly different speed. Different font sizes create visual hierarchy. The opposite directions create a dynamic tension that draws the eye.

Hover anywhere on the strip to freeze both rows. On houseofam.co.uk it would sit between the hero and the manifesto — a brand statement that never stops moving.
